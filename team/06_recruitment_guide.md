# AI Team Recruitment Guide
## Hiring Process, Interview Framework & Evaluation Criteria

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** February 2026  
**Purpose:** Standardize hiring process and ensure quality talent acquisition

---

## Recruitment Philosophy

**Core Principles:**
1. **Quality Over Speed:** Hire the right person, not the fastest
2. **Diverse Perspectives:** Build teams with varied backgrounds and experiences
3. **Skills-Based:** Focus on demonstrated abilities, not just credentials
4. **Cultural Add:** Look for people who enhance our culture, not just fit in
5. **Candidate Experience:** Respectful, transparent, and efficient process

**Hiring Goals:**
- Time to fill: 45-60 days (from req approval to offer acceptance)
- Offer acceptance rate: >80%
- New hire success rate (90-day): >95%
- Diversity targets: 40% women in technical roles

---

## Hiring Process Overview

### Standard Process (4-6 weeks)

```
Week 1: Sourcing & Screening
├── Day 1-3: Job posting, sourcing, referrals
├── Day 4-7: Resume screening (HR + Hiring Manager)
└── Output: 5-10 qualified candidates

Week 2: Initial Screens
├── 30-min phone screen (HR)
├── 30-min technical screen (Hiring Manager)
└── Output: 3-5 candidates advance

Week 3-4: Technical Interviews
├── 60-min technical deep-dive (Senior Engineer)
├── 60-min system design (Staff+ roles)
├── Take-home exercise (optional)
└── Output: 2-3 finalists

Week 5: Team & Culture Fit
├── 45-min peer interviews (2-3 team members)
├── 30-min culture fit (Cross-functional partner)
└── Output: 1-2 top candidates

Week 6: Executive & Offer
├── 30-min executive interview (VP/Director)
├── Reference checks (2-3)
├── Offer presentation & negotiation
└── Output: Hired candidate!
```

## Interview Evaluation Framework

### Scoring Rubric (1-5 Scale)

**5 - Exceptional:** Exceeds expectations, role model caliber  
**4 - Strong:** Meets all requirements, above average  
**3 - Acceptable:** Meets basic requirements, solid candidate  
**2 - Weak:** Below requirements, significant concerns  
**1 - Poor:** Does not meet requirements, not suitable

**Hiring Threshold:** Minimum 3.0 average across all categories, no category below 2.5

---

### Evaluation Categories

#### 1. Technical Competency (40% weight)

**AI Engineers:**
- **Coding Skills (0-5):** Code quality, efficiency, best practices
- **ML Knowledge (0-5):** Algorithm understanding, model selection, evaluation
- **System Design (0-5):** Architecture, scalability, integration (Senior+)
- **Domain Knowledge (0-5):** Energy/building understanding (nice-to-have)

**MLOps Engineers:**
- **Infrastructure (0-5):** Docker, Kubernetes, cloud platforms
- **CI/CD (0-5):** Pipeline design, automation, testing
- **MLOps Specific (0-5):** Model serving, monitoring, versioning
- **Security (0-5):** Best practices, compliance awareness

**Data Scientists:**
- **Statistics (0-5):** Hypothesis testing, experimental design
- **Analytics (0-5):** Data exploration, insight generation
- **Communication (0-5):** Storytelling, visualization, presentation
- **Business Acumen (0-5):** ROI understanding, value translation

#### 2. Problem-Solving (20% weight)

- **Analytical Thinking (0-5):** Breaks down complex problems systematically
- **Creativity (0-5):** Proposes innovative or non-obvious solutions
- **Decision Making (0-5):** Makes sound judgments with limited information
- **Learning Agility (0-5):** Quickly grasps new concepts and adapts

#### 3. Collaboration & Communication (20% weight)

- **Clarity (0-5):** Communicates ideas clearly and concisely
- **Listening (0-5):** Actively listens and incorporates feedback
- **Teamwork (0-5):** Demonstrates collaborative mindset and examples
- **Stakeholder Management (0-5):** Navigates different perspectives (Senior+)

#### 4. Cultural Fit & Values (20% weight)

- **Customer Obsession (0-5):** Shows genuine focus on customer value
- **Ownership (0-5):** Takes responsibility and drives outcomes
- **Continuous Learning (0-5):** Growth mindset and intellectual curiosity
- **Diversity & Inclusion (0-5):** Respects and values different perspectives

## Interview Questions Bank

### Technical Questions

**AI/ML Engineering:**

1. **Coding Exercise:**
   ```python
   # Given a time-series dataset with energy consumption and temperature,
   # implement a function to detect anomalies using z-score method.
   # Handle edge cases and optimize for performance.
   ```

2. **Algorithm Selection:**
   - "When would you use isolation forest vs. COPOD for anomaly detection?"
   - "How do you handle imbalanced datasets in classification?"
   - "Explain bias-variance tradeoff and how to manage it."

3. **System Design:**
   - "Design a real-time anomaly detection system for 1000 buildings."
   - "How would you handle model drift in production?"
   - "Design an A/B testing framework for ML models."

4. **Domain Knowledge:**
   - "What are common causes of energy waste in commercial buildings?"
   - "How would you validate that our model is actually saving energy?"
   - "Explain HVAC basics and how it impacts energy consumption."

**MLOps Engineering:**

1. **Infrastructure:**
   - "Design a Kubernetes deployment for a microservices-based ML application."
   - "How do you implement blue-green deployments for ML models?"
   - "Explain your approach to monitoring model performance in production."

2. **CI/CD:**
   - "Walk me through your ideal ML pipeline from code commit to production."
   - "How do you version datasets and ensure reproducibility?"
   - "What tests would you run before deploying a model?"

3. **Troubleshooting:**
   - "A model's latency suddenly increased 10x. How do you debug?"
   - "The training pipeline failed overnight. Walk me through your investigation."

**Data Science:**

1. **Statistics:**
   - "Explain p-values and why they can be misleading."
   - "How would you design an experiment to test if our model saves energy?"
   - "What's the difference between correlation and causation?"

2. **Analytics:**
   - "Given this dataset [provide sample], what insights would you look for?"
   - "How would you communicate technical findings to non-technical executives?"
   - "Design a dashboard to track model performance and business impact."

## Diversity & Inclusion in Hiring

### Sourcing Strategies

**Expand the Pipeline:**
- Partner with women-in-tech organizations (Girls in Tech, Women in ML)
- Engage with minority-serving institutions and HBCUs
- Post on diversity-focused job boards
- Attend diverse tech conferences and meetups
- Employee referral program with diversity bonuses

**Job Description Best Practices:**
- Use gender-neutral language (Textio or similar tools)
- Focus on required skills, not "nice-to-haves"
- Emphasize learning and growth opportunities
- Include diversity statement
- Avoid jargon and unnecessary requirements

**Example Inclusive Job Posting:**
```
We're looking for AI Engineers who are passionate about using 
technology to solve climate challenges. You don't need to know 
our entire stack - we value learning and growth. We especially 
encourage applications from underrepresented groups in tech.

Required: Python, ML fundamentals, problem-solving skills
Nice-to-have: Energy domain experience, cloud platforms
```

### Bias Mitigation

**Structured Interviews:**
- Same questions for all candidates
- Standardized evaluation rubrics
- Multiple interviewers (diverse panel when possible)
- Focus on behavioral examples (past performance)

**Blind Resume Review:**
- Remove names, photos, graduation years initially
- Focus on skills and experience
- Use structured scoring before revealing identity

**Training for Interviewers:**
- Unconscious bias training (mandatory)
- Calibration sessions with hiring team
- Regular review of hiring data for bias patterns

### Evaluation Adjustments

**Alternative Experience:**
- Value bootcamps, online courses, self-taught skills
- Consider non-traditional career paths
- Assess potential, not just past titles

**Accommodations:**
- Offer take-home exercises as alternative to live coding
- Provide interview questions in advance (for some rounds)
- Flexible interview formats (video, phone, in-person)
- Accommodations for disabilities or neurodiversity

## Offer & Negotiation

### Offer Package Components

**Base Salary:**
- Aligned with salary bands (see Role Definitions)
- Adjusted for location (Singapore vs. remote)
- Experience and interview performance factors

**Equity/Stock:**
- RSUs or stock options (role-dependent)
- Vesting schedule: 4 years with 1-year cliff
- Refresh grants for high performers

**Benefits:**
- Health insurance (family coverage)
- Retirement matching (up to 6%)
- Learning budget (SGD 3,000-5,000 annually)
- Flexible work arrangements
- Wellness benefits

**Sign-on Bonus:**
- For exceptional candidates or hard-to-fill roles
- Typically 10-20% of base salary
- Clawback if leave within 12 months

### Negotiation Guidelines

**Flexibility:**
- Base salary: ±10% from initial offer
- Sign-on bonus: Negotiable for competing offers
- Equity: Can increase for senior roles
- Benefits: Standardized, limited flexibility

**Red Lines:**
- Cannot exceed salary band maximum
- Must maintain internal equity
- Cannot compromise on visa/work authorization
- Background check must pass

**Competing Offers:**
- Ask for details (not just total comp)
- Match or beat on key components
- Emphasize non-monetary value (mission, growth, culture)
- Decision deadline: 5 business days standard
