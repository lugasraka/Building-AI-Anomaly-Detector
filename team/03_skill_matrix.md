## Overview

This document defines the technical and professional skills required for each role in the AI Solutions team, provides a framework for assessing current proficiency levels, and outlines personalized development paths for career growth.

**Skill Categories:**
1. **Technical Skills** - Domain-specific capabilities
2. **Professional Skills** - Communication, leadership, business acumen
3. **Domain Knowledge** - Industry and application expertise

**Proficiency Levels:**
- **Beginner (1):** Basic understanding, requires supervision
- **Intermediate (2):** Can work independently on standard tasks
- **Advanced (3):** Expert practitioner, can handle complex scenarios
- **Expert (4):** Thought leader, mentors others, drives innovation

## Individual Development Plans

### AI Engineer I (Junior → Mid-Level Path)

**Current Level:** Intermediate (2-3) in core skills  
**Target Level:** Advanced (3) within 12 months  
**Career Goal:** Promotion to AI Engineer II

#### Technical Development (70%)

**Q1 Focus: Strengthen ML Foundations**
- [ ] Complete "Machine Learning Specialization" (Coursera - Andrew Ng)
- [ ] Build 3 end-to-end ML projects with deployment
- [ ] Achieve AWS Certified Machine Learning - Specialty
- [ ] Mentor: Weekly 1:1 with AI Engineer II

**Q2 Focus: Production Systems**
- [ ] Lead deployment of 2 models to production
- [ ] Learn Docker and Kubernetes basics
- [ ] Implement comprehensive test suites (80%+ coverage)
- [ ] Shadow MLOps engineer for 2 weeks

**Q3 Focus: Advanced Techniques**
- [ ] Deep dive into time-series forecasting (Prophet, ARIMA, LSTM)
- [ ] Experiment with transformer models for anomaly detection
- [ ] Contribute to internal ML library
- [ ] Present at team Lunch & Learn

**Q4 Focus: Specialization**
- [ ] Choose specialization: Energy optimization OR Predictive maintenance
- [ ] Become team expert in chosen domain
- [ ] Lead architecture design for new project
- [ ] Mentor intern or junior engineer

#### Professional Development (20%)

- [ ] Improve presentation skills (Toastmasters or internal training)
- [ ] Write 2 technical blog posts
- [ ] Attend 2 industry conferences
- [ ] Build internal network (coffee chats with 10+ colleagues)

#### Domain Knowledge (10%)

- [ ] Complete "Building Energy Management" course
- [ ] Site visit to 3 customer buildings
- [ ] Read 5 industry whitepapers
- [ ] Shadow customer success manager for 1 week

**Success Criteria for Promotion:**
- 3+ production models deployed independently
- Code quality score >4.0/5.0
- Technical design ownership for 1 project
- Peer recognition as "go-to" person for specific domain

## Skills Assessment Process

### Quarterly Skills Review

**Self-Assessment (Week 1):**
1. Team member rates themselves on each skill (1-4 scale)
2. Identifies top 3 strengths and 3 development areas
3. Sets 2-3 learning goals for next quarter

**Manager Review (Week 2):**
1. Manager reviews self-assessment and provides feedback
2. Calibrates ratings based on observed performance
3. Discusses development opportunities
4. Aligns on learning goals and resources

**Peer Feedback (Week 3):**
1. 360-degree feedback from 3-4 peers
2. Focus on collaboration and knowledge sharing
3. Anonymous survey (5-10 questions)

**Development Plan Update (Week 4):**
1. Finalize Individual Development Plan (IDP)
2. Allocate training budget
3. Assign mentors or learning buddies
4. Schedule check-ins

### Annual Comprehensive Review

**Skills Gap Analysis:**
- Compare current skills vs. role requirements
- Identify team-wide skill gaps
- Plan hiring or training to address gaps

**Career Path Discussion:**
- Review long-term career aspirations
- Identify next role and requirements
- Create 2-3 year development roadmap

**Compensation Alignment:**
- Ensure skills are reflected in compensation
- Adjust for market rates
- Recognize skill acquisition

## Knowledge Sharing Program

### Weekly Lunch & Learn
- **Format:** 30-minute presentation + 15-min Q&A
- **Frequency:** Every Friday, 12:00-12:45 PM
- **Topics:** Technical deep-dives, project retrospectives, industry trends
- **Rotation:** Each team member presents 2x per year

### Monthly Paper Reading Club
- **Format:** Review 1 research paper or article
- **Discussion:** 1-hour group discussion
- **Focus:** Latest ML research, case studies, best practices
- **Output:** Summary document for team wiki

### Quarterly Hackathons
- **Format:** 24-hour innovation sprint
- **Teams:** Cross-functional groups of 3-4
- **Goals:** Experiment with new technologies, build prototypes
- **Prizes:** Recognition + budget for team celebration

### Internal Documentation
- **Confluence:** All learnings documented in team space
- **GitHub:** Code examples and templates
- **Wiki:** Runbooks, best practices, lessons learned
